What?

One of the things that many fitness-conscious people track is their consumption of macronutrients. You may have learned about "macros" during your biology class: carbohydrates, proteins, and fats.

Protein is absolutely essential for muscle growth, as it constitutes a large part of the muscle (just like water!). Carbs are important for providing energy - especially helpful during strenuous workouts.

And as ironic as it seems, you have to eat fat. Fat helps with anabolic hormone production (and energy storage). And definitely, definitely, definitely go for unsaturated fat over saturated fat. Olives, avocados, nuts, and legumes will always reign supreme over potato chips (in health but not taste).

When?

You can get everything right in terms of diet except timing and frequency and see diminished results. The bottom line is: eat five or six small meals throughout the day.

If you're trying to gain muscle, eating these multiple interspersed meals will allow you to reach your caloric goals. And if your goal is to lose fat, this pattern will help you not feel ravenous.

Some tips:

  • Eat your first meal as soon as you wake up.
  • Eat at about 3 hour intervals.
  • Before exercise, have a pre-workout drink or snack within an hour of starting and have another within an hour of completion.

Why?

The human body operates in interesting ways. The most important things you should consider when working out are how much you put in your body and how much is exerted through exercise.

This is the heart of nutrition. If you eat more than you exert, you are at a caloric surplus. This will direct the extra calories to grow more mass, and you will get bigger (hopefully with muscle). If you exert more than you eat, you will be at a caloric deficit, and will lose fat.

As for muscular content, the most important components are water and protein. This is the reason that protein is emphasized so much during weight gain - it is one of the building blocks for muscle! This is also the reason that people consume protein bars and protein shakes.

And don't forget about the building of block of not just muscles, but life. Muscles are primarily water, so drinking water is essential at all times. Recommended consumption is about 8 cups per day (as you've probably heard so many times), but it varies depending on person and activity level.
